ACQUIRING DESIGN

A Visualization of Cooper Hewitt Design Museum Collection

Discovering unexpected connections is easy when the related points of connections are readily available. Broader overview of collection rather than focused exhibitions. Drawing from the Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum's collection, Acquiring History filters the metadata of its objects to look closely at the relationship between the year a piece of design is created to when it is acquired to when it is exhibited. Furthermore, the project attempts to uncover trends when comparing the temporal data with the categorization of these design objects - such as medium, place of origin, artist/creator, acquisition by department, change in leadership, etc.

This is a side project by Stephanie Koltun.
